# DEFCON Map - Ethical Guidelines

## Core Principle

**This is not a game. Real people die in these conflicts.**

The DEFCON aesthetic is a visual hook, but the content is deadly serious. Every design decision must respect the human cost of war.

---

## Language Guidelines

### ❌ Never Use

| Phrase | Why |
|--------|-----|
| "Exciting developments" | War is not entertainment |
| "Level up" | No gamification |
| "Players" | This is not a game |
| "Winning" | No one wins in war |
| "Score" | Death is not a score |
| "Achievement unlocked" | Trivializes suffering |
| "Fun" | Not appropriate |
| "Game over" | Real people died |

### ✅ Always Use

| Phrase | Why |
|--------|-----|
| "Significant escalation" | Neutral, factual |
| "Users" or "Viewers" | Not players |
| "Track" or "Monitor" | Not "play" |
| "Civilian impact" | Human-centered |
| "Humanitarian crisis" | Reality |
| "Fatalities reported" | Honest language |

---

## Visual Guidelines

### ❌ Never

- Celebratory animations (confetti, cheering)
- "Achievement" popups
- Bright, playful colors
- Cartoony icons
- Reward sounds for escalations
- Leaderboards with rankings
- Betting/prediction mechanics

### ✅ Always

- Muted, serious color palette
- Minimalist, respectful icons
- Clear attribution to sources
- Fatality counts prominently displayed
- Links to humanitarian organizations
- Displacement numbers
- Civilian impact emphasized

---

## Data Handling

### Attribution

**Every data point must:**
- Link to original source
- Show timestamp
- Indicate confidence level
- Display methodology notes

**Example:**
```
Event: Explosion reported in Kyiv
Source: ACLED (verified)
Time: 2026-03-03 14:32 UTC
Confidence: High
Fatalities: 2 (confirmed)
```

### Accuracy

- **Never** report unverified claims as fact
- **Always** label unconfirmed reports
- **Correct** errors prominently
- **Delay** publication if verification pending

### Privacy

- Never identify individual civilians
- Aggregate data to protect identities
- Respect dignity of victims

## Monetization Ethics

### ✅ Acceptable

| Model | Why OK |
|-------|--------|
| Freemium API | Developers pay, public free |
| Enterprise licenses | Professional use |
| Sponsorship | If aligned (NGOs, news) |
| Donations | Community support |

### ❌ Unacceptable

| Model | Why Not OK |
|--------|-----------|
| Ads on conflict pages | Profiting from suffering |
| Paywalled alerts | Safety shouldn't be paid |
| "Premium" conflict access | Information for all |
| Betting integration | Never |
| NFTs of conflicts | Absolutely not |

### Revenue Sharing

**Strongly consider:**
- Donating % of revenue to humanitarian aid
- Partnering with NGOs (IRC, MSF, etc.)
- Transparent reporting on donations

## Content Guidelines

### What to Show

- ✅ Conflict events (verified)
- ✅ Fatalities (aggregated)
- ✅ Displacement numbers
- ✅ Humanitarian access issues
- ✅ Civilian impact
- ✅ International responses

### What NOT to Show

- ❌ Graphic violence
- ❌ Individual victim identification
- ❌ Propaganda (from any side)
- ❌ Unverified social media claims
- ❌ Military operational details
- ❌ Troop movements (safety)

---

## Responding to Criticism

**Expect criticism.** Some will say this trivializes war.

**Response:**

> "We understand the concern. This tool is designed to raise awareness about global conflicts, not to entertain. Every design decision prioritizes accuracy, attribution, and respect for victims. We link to humanitarian organizations and emphasize civilian impact. Our goal is to make conflict data accessible and visible, not to gamify suffering."

---

## Red Lines (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Never celebrate escalations**
2. **Never gamify civilian casualties**
3. **Never allow betting or predictions for profit**
4. **Never present as entertainment**
5. **Never remove human context**
6. **Never profit from increased conflict**
7. **Never show graphic violence**
8. **Never identify individual victims**

---

## Review Checklist

Before any public release:

- [ ] No gamification language
- [ ] Human cost emphasized
- [ ] Sources attributed
- [ ] Links to aid organizations
- [ ] Serious visual tone
- [ ] No celebratory animations
- [ ] Accuracy verified
- [ ] Privacy protected
- [ ] Ethical guidelines documented

## Inspiration

**The Doomsday Clock** has been respected for 75+ years despite being essentially a "threat meter." It works because:

- Serious presentation
- Scientific backing
- Clear methodology
- No entertainment framing
- Focus on awareness, not engagement

**Emulate this approach.**

---

*These guidelines are non-negotiable. If any feature violates these principles, it doesn't ship.*
